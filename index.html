Practice & Project Management · PC 1–16
PC 1 Regulatory Requirements & Obligations Understand regulatory requirements and obligations pertaining to practice as an architect, including professional codes of conduct and obligations for CPD and PII. Demonstrate understanding of regulatory requirements, including legislation, codes of conduct, and obligations for CPD and PII. Comply with all regulatory requirements and obligations pertaining to practice as an architect, including legislation, codes of conduct, CPD and PII. Awareness of the ethical criteria embedded in Architects Acts, the Architects Award, National Employment Standards and other relevant legislation (e.g. Racial Discrimination Act 1975, Sex Discrimination Act 1984).
PC 2 Quality Assurance Systems Understand the role of quality assurance systems in facilitating efficient and timely delivery of architectural services. Identify practice resources and apply practice methods and QA systems within an ethical practice management framework to facilitate consistent delivery. Implement practice resources and apply ethical employment practice methods and QA systems to facilitate efficient, consistent and timely delivery of architectural services. Explore the nature of quality assurance systems as applied to delivering standard architectural services through examination of a scenario or case study.
PC 3 Project Planning Understand the principles of project planning, considering implications for Country, environmental sustainability, communities, stakeholders and project costs. Demonstrate understanding of the principles of project planning, considering implications for Country, environmental sustainability, communities, stakeholders and project costs. Apply principles of project planning, considering implications for Country, environmental sustainability, communities, stakeholders and project costs. Identify the phases of a project from inception to completion, identify tasks requiring external input, and describe deliverables — including due diligence on cultural heritage overlays, First Nations history and rights, and community/stakeholder groups.
PC 4 Project Resource Planning Be able to apply principles of project and staff planning and resource costs to establish realistic and achievable time frames. (Applies at registration and post-registration.) At registration/post-registration: preparation of a project resourcing schedule covering phases of service, staff roles, time estimates and staff costs including overheads and profit.
PC 5 Client Architect Agreement Understand the essential elements of a client architect agreement across the range of procurement methods and different scales and types of project. Demonstrate understanding of essential elements of a CAA and be able to explain appropriateness of different agreements in relation to scale and type, including partial services and secondary/sub-consultants. Apply essential elements of a CAA across the range of procurement methods in relation to their appropriateness to the scale and type of the project. Develop a draft Client Architect Agreement, with appropriate recognition of the particular project type, fee structure and architectural services undertaken in each phase.
PC 6 Scope of Services Variations Understand appropriate processes for reporting and varying the scope of services provided by an architect. Demonstrate understanding of appropriate processes for reporting and varying scope of services. Apply appropriate processes for reporting and varying the scope of services provided by an architect. Develop a draft Client Architect Agreement, with appropriate recognition of the contract terms to vary the scope of services included in the agreement.
PC 7 Client / Stakeholder Communications & Approvals Understand appropriate processes for clear and consistent communication with clients and relevant stakeholders, including obtaining approvals. Apply and follow processes for clear and consistent communication with clients and relevant stakeholders throughout the project, including obtaining approvals. (Registration & post-registration.) Demonstrate awareness of the obligation to keep the client and stakeholders informed and seek approval embedded in the relevant Architects Acts and codes of conduct, and awareness of appropriate modes of communication.
PC 8 First Nations Engagement Processes Understand how to implement culturally responsive and meaningful engagement processes that respect the importance of Country and reciprocal relationships with Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Peoples. Be able to implement culturally responsive and meaningful engagement processes that respect the importance of Country and reciprocal relationships with Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Peoples. (Registration & post-registration.) Engage with First Nations authored books, articles and audio-visuals to build understanding of social, cultural and historical contexts. Write a culturally responsive engagement plan for a studio project.
PC 9 Procurement Methods Understand contemporary and emerging building procurement methods — including identifying appropriate delivery forms, risks, mitigation/adaptation strategies, and appropriate contracts and agreements. Demonstrate understanding and identify the most appropriate delivery form for a project, integrating appropriate construction contracts and consultancy agreements. Apply contemporary and emerging procurement methods, identifying the most appropriate delivery form, risks, mitigation strategies and contracts. Explore the advantages and disadvantages of various procurement methods (fixed lump sum, D&C, cost plus, turnkey) and map them against stages of architectural services.
PC 10 Whole Life Carbon Understand the whole life carbon implications of procurement methods, materials, components and construction systems. Demonstrate understanding of the WLC implications of procurement methods, materials, components and construction systems. (Registration & post-registration.) Explain the key concepts of embodied and operational carbon through exploration of a case study or design report that considers WLC with commentary on building materials, components and/or construction systems.
PC 11 Procurement Process Assess, recommend and/or select an appropriate procurement process, considering its impact on all phases of a project — design, documentation and delivery — and advise the client on scope of service for consultants. (Registration & post-registration.) At registration/post-registration: explain key information to deliver a medium–large commercial development under design and construct — describing how design, documentation and delivery services are provided and the expected scope for structural and services engineers.
PC 12 Statutory Requirements / Building Codes Understand how relevant building codes, standards and planning controls apply across architectural practice, including climate change implications, fire safety, and universal access. Provide independent, culturally responsive and objective advice in accordance with relevant building codes, standards, technical specifications and planning regulations, including climate change implications. (Registration & post-registration.) Prepare a concept design for a building typology with specific planning or siting controls, with design responses that include core themes such as climate change, fire egress and universal access.
PC 13 Documentation Processes Have knowledge of documentation processes that facilitate project delivery appropriate to selected procurement processes. Identify and apply strategies, programming and processes for documentation through all project stages to facilitate project delivery, as appropriate to selected procurement processes. (Registration & post-registration.) Demonstrate awareness of the different types of documentation required under each phase of architectural service, explaining their purpose and the external processes required — and map changes in documentation delivery between lump sum and D&C procurement.
PC 14 Construction Services / Contract Admin Systems Identify and apply construction services provisions and/or construction administration systems needed to fulfil all obligations appropriate to the procurement process in accordance with the terms of the agreement. (Registration & post-registration.) At registration/post-registration: identify the activities required for construction phase services, describe appropriate processes/documentation for executing obligations under the contract, and establish template documents and filing systems.
PC 15 Copyright & Moral Rights Understand legal and ethical obligations relating to copyright, moral rights, authorship of cultural knowledge and intellectual property requirements across architectural services. Comply with legal and ethical obligations relating to copyright, moral rights, authorship of cultural knowledge and intellectual property requirements. (Registration & post-registration.) Undertake a cultural risk assessment in a studio project or written work to demonstrate understanding of legal and ethical obligations relating to copyright, moral rights, IP and authorship of cultural knowledge.
PC 16 Risk Management Understand risk management and mitigation principles and strategies across architectural services — including safety in design, project risk, resilience from climate change impacts and appropriate insurances. Apply risk management and mitigation strategies across architectural services — including safety in design, project risk, resilience from climate change impacts and appropriate insurances. (Registration & post-registration.) Examine case studies describing a risk management process with mitigation strategies for climate change impacts on a building, and analyse designers' obligations for safety in design with examples of hazards and risk minimisation for a simple building typology.
Project Initiation & Conceptual Design · PC 17–35
PC 17 Caring for Country Have an understanding of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Peoples' aspirations to care for Country and how these inform architectural design. (All levels.) Identify opportunities to partner with First Nations communities to inform projects about their visions for Country, or engage with First Nations authored resources. Demonstrate through a report or case study.
PC 18 Iterative Concept Design Processes Apply creative imagination, design precedents, research, emergent knowledge and critical evaluation in formulating and refining concept design options, including exploration of three-dimensional form and spatial quality. (All levels.) Prepare concept design options employing appropriate presentation techniques that explore responses to specific project criteria (brief, budget, site analysis, planning controls, client aspirations) and evaluate them using comparative measures.
PC 19 Project Feasibility Assessments Understand the purpose of project feasibility assessments, including research of site constraints, opportunities and risks, and methods of determining preliminary cost analysis. Identify, analyse and evaluate client project requirements and objectives using qualitative and quantitative methods and, where required, assist cost estimators in determining project feasibility/viability. Prepare a portfolio tracking the design process for a studio project — including detailed site analysis, planning control research, evaluation of applicable building typologies, and exploring how architects describe projects through area scheduling and published cost per m² rates.
PC 20 Project Budgets Assess project budget and timeframe against project requirements and objectives, relevant legislation, statutory planning requirements, building codes and standards. (Registration & post-registration.) At registration/post-registration: identify legislative, statutory and building code requirements specific to a project that would create additional cost or programming requirements (e.g. bushfire zone site).
PC 21 Establishing Project Budgets Apply project budgets, or work with a quantity surveyor to establish project budgets, based upon understanding of cost planning, value management and factors influencing project cost. (Registration & post-registration.) At registration/post-registration: prepare feasibility studies and concept documentation exploring responses to specific criteria and evaluate them using comparative area schedules or qualitative descriptors to assist cost consultants determine project budget.
PC 22 Conflicts of Interest Identify and manage risks arising from real or perceived conflicts of interest. (Registration & post-registration.) At registration/post-registration: define what constitutes a real and perceived conflict of interest and describe scenarios where a conflict of interest could arise.
PC 23 Return Brief Understand the purpose and process of generating a return brief for approval by the client and relevant stakeholders, including awareness of the implications of non-compliance. Prepare a return brief for approval by the client and relevant stakeholders in response to a client brief and any areas of deviation or non-compliance. (Registration & post-registration.) Examine the component parts of a return brief and analyse why certain information is included and how it shapes the development of a project.
PC 24 Project Development Options Understand how to identify and evaluate project development options in response to a project brief — objectives, budget, user intent, built purpose, risks, timeframe and environmental sustainability. Prepare and analyse project development options in response to a project brief — objectives, budget, user intent, built purpose, risks, timeframes and environmental sustainability. Present a portfolio tracking the design process for a studio project, showing generation of alternative options, their rigorous assessment and subsequent design responses.
PC 25 History & Theory of Architecture Draw on knowledge from the history and theory of architecture as part of preliminary design research and when developing the conceptual design. (All levels.) Prepare concept design studies, diagrams and sketches that encompass and express historical and contemporary thinking — including how social, cultural and ethical values may be addressed through case studies, precedents and typologies.
PC 26 Site, Cultural & Contextual Analysis Undertake site, cultural and contextual analysis as part of preliminary design research. (All levels.) Use project work to describe how physical, environmental, social and historical aspects of a site were understood through research of site history, cultural observations, contextual analysis and synthesis of findings.
PC 27 First Nations Engagement (Concept) Understand how to embed the knowledge, worldviews and perspectives of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Peoples, shared through engagement, into the conceptual design in a meaningful, respectful and appropriate way. (All levels.) Engage critically with First Nations' designs, literature and projects; use culturally responsible language in spoken and written contexts, embedded into a conceptual design.
PC 28 Preliminary Design Research Draw on knowledge from building sciences and technology, environmental sciences and behavioural and social sciences as part of preliminary design research and when developing the conceptual design to optimise project performance. (All levels.) Develop a return brief including design criteria born of research into building sciences, environmental sciences and behavioural/social sciences, and prepare a concept design report evaluating options against that criteria.
PC 29 Evaluation of Design Options Develop and evaluate design options in terms of the heritage, cultural and community values embodied in the site, and in relation to project requirements. (All levels.) Present a studio project or oral/written report examining the role of broad site analysis in establishing design parameters, including research of historical/cultural influences and how architects respond to community needs.
PC 30 Project Siting Explore options for siting a project, including integrating information and analysis of relevant cultural, social and economic factors. (All levels.) Prepare site analysis diagrams describing opportunities and constraints, with evaluation of criteria as a basis for decisions about preferred project siting.
PC 31 Environmental Sustainability Identify, analyse and integrate information relevant to environmental sustainability — energy and water consumption, resources depletion, waste, embodied carbon and carbon emissions — over the life cycle of a project. (All levels.) Prepare a case study or design report considering a building's environmental performance over lifecycle stages, analysing relevant factors for the context, brief, climate and building type (e.g. carbon, waste, energy, water).
PC 32 Planning Principles / Statutory Planning Apply planning principles and statutory planning requirements to the site and conceptual design of the project. (All levels.) Prepare a report or feasibility study for a specific site by researching and applying statutory planning controls. Design response should include consideration of site constraints and planning principles informing the building envelope.
PC 33 Integration of Sustainable Environmental Systems Investigate, coordinate and integrate sustainable environmental systems — water, thermal, lighting and acoustics — into the conceptual design. Investigate, coordinate and integrate sustainable environmental systems — water, thermal, lighting and acoustics — in response to consultants' advice. Present a studio project or case study investigating a range of environmental systems (water, thermal, lighting, acoustics), defining the required specialist consultants and exploring how those systems are integrated into a concept design.
PC 34 Communication with Stakeholders Apply principles and methodologies for presenting conceptual design proposals to clients, stakeholders and communities, using culturally responsive methods appropriate to different audiences. Communicate conceptual design proposals using appropriate and culturally responsive methods appropriate to different audiences. (Registration & post-registration.) Learn about the key concepts of cultural responsiveness — identifying cultural biases and examining appropriate language and presentation techniques to convey design ideas to different audiences.
PC 35 Operational & Embodied Carbon Understand the operational and embodied carbon implications of chosen materials, components and systems. Assess the operational and embodied carbon implications of materials, components, construction systems and supply chains (including transport) to achieve net zero whole life carbon when developing design concepts. Prepare reports, case studies or design exercises that consider the operational and embodied carbon of a building, including commentary on how different design decisions, materials or systems impact carbon outcomes.
Detailed Design & Construction Documentation · PC 36–47
PC 36 Engagement with First Nations Peoples Apply creative imagination, design precedents, emergent knowledge, critical evaluation and continued engagement with Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Peoples to produce a coherent project design. (All levels.) Identify many forms of cultural representation and expression in projects (spatial, material, cultural practices). Engage with First Nations authored resources to raise self-awareness of values and laws, and demonstrate these learnings through the detailed design stage.
PC 37 Documentation Appropriate for Construction Produce timely, accurate, complete and comprehensible documentation of the design so that it can be constructed. (Registration & post-registration.) At registration/post-registration: prepare IFC documents (drawings, specifications, schedules) with appropriate level of detail — e.g. setout drawings, door/window schedules, reflected ceiling plans, statutory signage schedules.
PC 38 Value Management Processes Work within budget and time constraints while maintaining the defined project design intent, including participating in value management processes where engaged. (Registration & post-registration.) At registration/post-registration: prepare design options that explore responses to budget/program criteria, evaluate using comparative area schedules, and prepare design options based on costing feedback as part of a value management exercise.
PC 39 Material Selections / Structural & Construction Systems Understand how the integration of material selection, structural and construction systems impacts on design outcomes. Integrate the material selection, structural and construction systems established in the conceptual design into the detailed design and documentation. Present case studies or a studio design project developing concept sketches to developed design documentation, demonstrating understanding of structural considerations, materials, components and systems.
PC 40 Design Presentations to Stakeholders Resolve and present a coherent detailed design solution within necessary timeframes to obtain client and stakeholder approvals. (All levels.) Explore what type of information requires client/stakeholder approval and identify the most appropriate forms of presenting this information in the detailed design phase, as part of a studio design project with detailed design deliverables.
PC 41 Consultant Coordination Coordinate and integrate input from specialists and consultants into the detailed design and documentation. (Registration & post-registration.) At registration/post-registration: prepare architectural drawings integrating consultant information — e.g. BIM modelling with structural/services and clash detection, concrete setout drawings showing services penetrations, coordinated reflected ceiling plans.
PC 42 Planning Applications Prepare planning applications that comply with planning regulations. (Registration & post-registration.) At registration/post-registration: prepare planning application documentation including a Planning Report to support the submission.
PC 43 Early Contractor Involvement Collaborate with nominated contractors early in the documentation process to identify key construction methodology opportunities and constraints. (Registration & post-registration.) At registration/post-registration: identify the process for engaging with the ECI contractor and define the impact on architectural services to accommodate an ECI process.
PC 44 Consultants Understand the roles and types of relevant consultants and suppliers as well as applicable construction terminology. Maintain effective and clear communication in the coordination of relevant consultants, manufacturers and suppliers as required under terms of engagement. Identify which specialist consultants would be required for a given scenario, and identify architect and consultant responsibilities when preparing a Client Architect Agreement or consultant agreement.
PC 45 Quality & Performance Standards Understand processes for selecting materials, finishes, fittings, components and systems, based on quality and performance standards, impact on Country and the environment, and whole life carbon. Nominate and integrate quality and performance standards for selected materials, finishes, fittings, components and systems, considering impact on Country, the environment and whole life carbon. Integrate LCAs and specialist advice. Prepare specifications in a studio project establishing a rationale for each material, finish, fitting or component — including impacts on Country and beyond the carbon footprint.
PC 46 Project Documentation Understand processes for producing project documentation that meets the requirements of the contract and procurement procedure, and complies with regulatory controls and building standards. Produce project documentation that meets the requirements of the contract and procurement procedure, and complies with regulatory controls and building standards. Map the various architectural documentation deliverables against milestones inherent in different procurement modes, and explore the different documentation types required for a particular phase of services.
PC 47 Accurate Documents Complete and communicate on-time, accurate documents for relevant stakeholders, including drawings, models, specifications, schedules, and construction documentation. (All levels.) Present a portfolio of design drawings, model photographs and reports/specifications, showing examples of communication techniques and processes appropriate for a particular design stage.
Design Delivery & Construction Phase Services · PC 48–60
PC 48 Procurement Method Implications for Project Delivery Understand available procurement methods and their application to project delivery, considering project type, scale and contractor coordination. Select and implement project administration systems, based upon assessment of the selected procurement method and its implications on project delivery. Analyse and evaluate common procurement methodologies in terms of their impact on time, cost and quality — describing the risk allocation for each and determining when one method may be favoured over another.
PC 49 Project Team Structures Implement project team structures necessary to deliver a full suite of professional services or partial services appropriate to the selected procurement process. (Registration & post-registration.) At registration/post-registration: describe the roles and responsibilities of a project team and articulate how they change over the different phases of architectural service.
PC 50 Continued Engagement with First Nations Peoples Continue engagement with relevant Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Peoples throughout all stages of the project and its delivery in a meaningful, respectful and appropriate way. (Registration & post-registration.) At registration/post-registration: embed Traditional Custodians' values in the project values and/or embed processes in the QA system that track engagement from beginning to end — potentially including a "cultural project diary".
PC 51 Contractor Selection Understand the process of selecting qualified contractors in accordance with an agreed procurement method and construction contract. Provide advice to clients and lead or contribute to the process of selecting a qualified contractor in accordance with the agreed procurement method and construction contract. Examine the tender process for a lump sum contract and prepare an Information to Tender letter referencing the selected construction contract and using appropriate terminology.
PC 52 Contractual Relationship Defined by Procurement Method Apply the principles and mechanisms implicit in the selected procurement method and associated construction contract(s), based on an understanding of the implications of differing contractual relationships. (Registration & post-registration.) At registration/post-registration: analyse and evaluate the risk allocation for each party under common procurement methodologies, understand the effect of novation, and determine circumstances in which one method may be favoured over another.
PC 53 Advice to Client on Procurement Methods Provide advice to clients on the impact of a selected procurement method on cost, time, life cycle implications and quality control during construction. (Registration & post-registration.) At registration/post-registration: prepare a report articulating the advantages and disadvantages of various procurement strategies weighted against specific project criteria to enable the client to make an informed decision.
PC 54 Periodic Site Visits Understand the purpose of periodic site visits of construction works for quality assurance. Monitor construction progress and quality as required under the provisions of the construction contract, which may include site visits. Examine an architect's duty of care or obligations to the client under a standard form of contract (e.g. a simple lump sum contract).
PC 55 Record Keeping & Document Control Understand methodologies for record keeping, document control and revision status during the construction phase. Apply appropriate and consistent systems for record keeping, document control and revision status during the construction phase. Explore architectural documentation standards, methods for controlling documents and the consequences of not appropriately controlling documents.
PC 56 Identification of Defects Understand the purpose of identification of defects, rectifications and approval of substitutions. Apply appropriate and consistent systems for identification of defects, rectifications and approval of substitutions. Explore the legal and ethical framework applicable to architects for identifying building defects and approving building element substitutions.
PC 57 Contract Administration Understand the principles of contract administration, including certification, variations, instructions, RFIs and practical completion. Apply relevant processes required for certification of monetary progress claims, project variations, extensions of time, project instructions, RFIs, practical completion and other administrative functions explicit in the construction contract. Examine the processes in a standard form contract (e.g. simple lump sum) — applying liquidated damages, issuing a progress claim with cash retention, or issuing an extension of time claim in response to a given scenario.
PC 58 Contract Documents for Contract Completion Understand the contract components and the process of executing a contract, as defined within the construction contract and in accordance with relevant building and planning codes. Complete documentation — specifications, drawings, schedules, reports, certification and approvals — and other project information for issue to the client and relevant authorities, as required under the construction contract. Complete a lump sum contract with list of contract documents using a studio project as the basis for project information, and identify which documents are required to obtain a certificate of occupancy.
PC 59 Preparing & Recording Documentation Understand and mitigate risks associated with preparing and recording documentation. (Registration & post-registration.) At registration/post-registration: apply quality assurance processes across a project to manage and monitor project documentation, control documentation quality, and mitigate risks in completing compliant project documentation.
PC 60 Post Occupancy Evaluation Apply appropriate methodologies for undertaking post occupancy evaluations and life cycle assessment where required under terms of engagement. (Registration & post-registration.) At registration/post-registration: participate in a POE led by a specialist, including conducting background research using existing documentation, gathering field data, and analysing data against assessment criteria.
